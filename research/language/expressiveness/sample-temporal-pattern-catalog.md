# Temporal Pattern Catalog — Issue-43 Sample Corpus**Research date:** 2026-04-13**Author:** George (Runtime Dev)**Relevance:** Empirical domain evidence for NodaTime type alignment (cross-ref: `nodatime-type-model.md`, `nodatime-precept-alignment.md`)---## SummaryAnalysis of 15 sample `.precept` files on `feature/issue-43-sample-realism-rollout` reveals **91 explicit `FUTURE(date)` markers** and **29 integer surrogate fields** encoding temporal values. The samples span insurance, finance, healthcare, IT operations, procurement, HR, library circulation, maintenance, and security — a representative cross-section of business domains.**Key findings:**1. **Date (LocalDate) dominates:** ~70 of the 91 FUTURE markers are pure calendar dates (filing dates, effective dates, approval dates). The `date` type from proposal #26 alone covers the majority.2. **Time-of-day (LocalTime) is rare but real:** 2 fields in clinic-appointment-scheduling (AppointmentTime, CheckInTime). Low frequency but impossible to model with `date` alone.3. **Duration/Period is the second-largest category (~19 surrogates):** Grace periods, loan terms, PM frequencies, warranty remaining, days-since counters, estimated hours. These drive the most awkward workarounds — day-counter simulation events (`AdvanceLapseClock`, `AdvanceCancellationClock`, `AdvanceDay`) that exist solely because the DSL lacks temporal arithmetic.4. **Deadline/Window is a computed pattern, not a type:** ~10 fields are "date + period = deadline" (ExpirationDate, AppealDeadline, MaturityDate, DueDate, SlaTargetDate). These need date arithmetic, not a new type.5. **DateTime/Instant appears in 1 sample:** security-incident uses "At" suffixed timestamps (DetectedAt, ContainedAt, etc.) suggesting date+time precision. This is the weakest signal — 7 markers in 1 sample.6. **3 samples contain elaborate day-counter simulation machinery** (library-book-checkout, insurance-policy-lifecycle, subscription-cancellation-retention) that would collapse to trivial date expressions with native date + period types.| Category | FUTURE markers | Integer surrogates | Total occurrences ||---|---|---|---|| Date (calendar date) | ~70 | 7 | ~77 || Time (time-of-day) | 2 | 2 | 4 || Duration (elapsed time) | 1 | 8 | 9 || Period (calendar span) | 0 | 10 | 10 || Deadline/Window (computed) | ~10 | 0 | ~10 || DateTime/Instant | 7 | 0 | 7 |---## Per-Sample Analysis### 1. asset-record-lifecycle.precept**FUTURE(date) markers (6):**| Marker | Target field/arg | Intended type | Pattern ||---|---|---|---|| AcquisitionDate | new field | Date | Calendar date || WarrantyExpirationDate | new field | Date | Deadline/Window (Acquisition + warranty term) || CommissionDate | new field | Date | Calendar date || LastMaintenanceDate | new field | Date | Calendar date || DecommissionDate | new field | Date | Calendar date || DisposalDate | new field | Date | Calendar date |**Temporal surrogates (1):**| Field | Current type | Intended type | Pattern ||---|---|---|---|| `WarrantyRemaining` | `integer` | Period (months or days) | Duration/Period — remaining warranty countdown |**Notes:** Straightforward date-stamping lifecycle. Day-Counter Simulation Events (3 samples)**Samples:** library-book-checkout, insurance-policy-lifecycle, subscription-cancellation-retentionThese three samples share an identical workaround pattern:- An integer field tracks elapsed days (`DaysSinceLapse`, `DaysSinceCancellation`, `CurrentDay`)- A manual "advance clock" event increments the counter (`AdvanceLapseClock`, `AdvanceCancellationClock`, `AdvanceDay`)- Guards compare the counter to a period threshold (`DaysSinceLapse <= GracePeriodDays`)This pattern is **pure DSL ceremony** — it exists because the language lacks temporal arithmetic. With `date` + `period`:- The counter field becomes a computed field: `DaysSinceLapse -> today() - LapseDate`- The advance event disappears entirely- The guard becomes: `LapseDate + GracePeriodDays >= today()`**Impact:** Eliminates ~30 lines of boilerplate per sample and removes events that have no domain meaning.### 2. Month-Granularity Periods (3 samples)**Samples:** insurance-policy-lifecycle (`TermLengthMonths`), loan-application (`LoanTermMonths`), preventive-maintenance-templateThese fields measure calendar spans in months. NodaTime's `Period` handles this correctly; `Duration` does not (it counts fixed nanoseconds and cannot represent "3 months" because months have variable lengths). This is the strongest argument for the `Period` vs. `Duration` distinction in the NodaTime alignment analysis.### 3. Duration Accumulation (2 samples)**Samples:** maintenance-work-order (`ActualHours += RecordLabor.Hours`), preventive-maintenance-template (`EstimatedDurationMinutes`)Work-hour tracking requires sub-day duration with accumulation. Currently modeled as `decimal`, which loses temporal semantics.### 4. Time-of-Day with Arithmetic (1 sample)**Sample:** clinic-appointment-scheduling (`AppointmentMinuteOfDay % 15 == 0`)The 15-minute slot alignment invariant requires modular arithmetic on time values. A `time` type would need to support this or the invariant would need to use a different expression form.### 5. Full Timestamps (1 sample)**Sample:** security-incident (`DetectedAt`, `TriagedAt`, etc.)Incident response needs date+time precision. This is a single domain but a real one. Could be modeled as `date` + `time` pair, or requires a `datetime` composite type.---## Strongest Domain Evidence### Tier 1 — Immediate demand (date + period)**library-book-checkout** is the strongest single case. It has 5 integer surrogates, a global day-simulation event that appears in 6+ `from` clauses, date subtraction (`DaysOverdue -> CurrentDay - DueDay`), date addition (`DueDay + ExtraDays`), and date comparison in guards and state constraints. Native `date` + `period` would eliminate approximately 40% of the precept's transition logic.**insurance-policy-lifecycle** is the broadest case. 9 FUTURE(date) markers, 3 surrogates across 2 granularities (days and months), a grace-period simulation event, and a lapse-reinstatement workflow that is entirely time-gated. The month-granularity `TermLengthMonths` field is the strongest argument for NodaTime `Period` over raw `Duration`.**subscription-cancellation-retention** is the most common business pattern (SaaS/recurring billing). The reinstatement-window logic mirrors insurance-policy-lifecycle's lapse pattern, confirming this is a recurring archetype, not a one-off.### Tier 2 — Important but lower frequency**preventive-maintenance-template** has the richest variety of temporal types (3 periods of different granularity, 2 durations in different units, 1 meter-based trigger). It's the best stress-test for a temporal type system.**clinic-appointment-scheduling** is the only sample requiring `time` type. The 15-minute alignment constraint is a real scheduling domain requirement but appears in only this one sample.### Tier 3 — Edge case for current scope**security-incident** is the only sample requiring `datetime`. Real need (NIST compliance), but a single domain. Could be deferred or modeled as a pair.### ConclusionThe corpus makes a strong empirical case for shipping `date` first (#26) but simultaneously designing the type system with `period` and `duration` in mind. The day-counter simulation workaround (3 samples, ~90 lines of boilerplate) is the most compelling evidence that `date`-only is insufficient for real domain models — period types are the next highest-value addition. Time-of-day and datetime are real but lower-priority based on frequency.
