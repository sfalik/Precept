# Philosophy Research

Research that grounds Precept's product philosophy in external evidence, domain analysis, and positioning rationale.

Philosophy research is distinct from language research. Language research informs syntax and construct design. Philosophy research informs *why Precept exists*, *what it governs*, and *how it positions relative to adjacent tools* — the durable claims that shape the product narrative.

## Start here

- [`entity-first-positioning-evidence.md`](entity-first-positioning-evidence.md) — Evidence base for entity-first framing, states-are-optional, and category positioning against validators, state machines, policy engines, and enterprise record-model platforms.

## What belongs here

- Durable positioning rationale grounded in external evidence
- Cross-cutting philosophical conclusions promoted from domain-specific research
- Category analysis that informs how Precept describes itself
- Evidence that strengthens or challenges claims in `docs/philosophy.md`

## What does NOT belong here

- Sample rosters, corpus planning, or rewrite sequencing — those belong in `docs/research/sample-realism/`
- Language syntax proposals or construct design — those belong in `docs/research/language/`
- Brand voice or visual identity — those belong in `design/brand/`

## Relationship to `docs/philosophy.md`

`docs/philosophy.md` is the canonical philosophy document — concise, declarative, product-facing. It states what Precept is and why.

This folder holds the *evidence* behind those claims. Philosophy.md links here when a claim benefits from supporting research. The research folder is never a substitute for the philosophy doc — it is the foundation the philosophy doc stands on.
